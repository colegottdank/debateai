But not all topics make for great AI debates. Here are the ones that produce the best practice sessions in 2026.

## Best Topics for Beginners

These topics have clear two-sided structures and enough accessible evidence that you won't get lost.

### 1. Should School Start Later?
**Why it's great for AI debate:** The evidence is genuinely split. Sleep science says yes, logistics and extracurriculars say it's complicated. The AI will make you engage with both.

**What the AI will throw at you:** If you argue for later start times, expect pushback on transportation costs, afterschool activity impacts, and parent work schedules. If you argue against, expect a wall of sleep research.

### 2. Is Homework Necessary?
**Why it's great:** Almost every student has an opinion. But the AI will force you past "I don't like it" into actual arguments about learning outcomes, equity (not all students have good home environments), and diminishing returns.

### 3. Should College Be Free?
**Why it's great:** Economics meets values. The AI is strong on both sides here — it can argue the investment case for free college AND the fiscal responsibility case against it with equal conviction.

## Best Topics for Intermediate Debaters

These require more nuance and the ability to handle multi-layered arguments.

### 4. Should AI Be Used in Criminal Sentencing?
**Why it's great for 2026:** This is happening *right now*. AI risk assessment tools are used in courts. The debate touches on bias, fairness, due process, and the limits of algorithmic decision-making. Extremely current.

**AI strength:** The AI will cite actual cases where AI sentencing tools showed racial bias, then pivot to argue that human judges are ALSO biased — and at least AI bias is measurable.

### 5. Is Social Media a Net Positive for Democracy?
**Why it's great:** Goes beyond "social media bad" into real tradeoffs. Arab Spring vs. January 6th. Information access vs. misinformation. Grassroots organizing vs. polarization.

### 6. Should Gene Editing Be Allowed on Human Embryos?
**Why it's great:** Science, ethics, and philosophy in one topic. The AI handles the medical case (eliminating genetic diseases) and the slippery slope argument (designer babies, eugenics) with equal force.

### 7. Does Economic Growth Justify Environmental Damage?
**Why it's great:** Forces you to quantify tradeoffs instead of staying abstract. The AI will make you deal with real numbers — GDP impacts, carbon costs, developing nation perspectives.

## Best Topics for Advanced Debaters

These are philosophically complex and require genuine critical thinking to navigate.

### 8. Is Privacy a Fundamental Right or a Social Construct?
**Why it's great:** The AI will challenge the very framework of your argument. If you say "fundamental right," it'll ask you to define what makes a right fundamental. If you say "social construct," it'll push on what that means for surveillance.

### 9. Should Developed Nations Accept Unlimited Climate Refugees?
**Why it's great:** Morality vs. practicality. International law vs. sovereignty. Historical responsibility vs. current capacity. This topic has no clean answer, which makes it perfect for advanced practice.

### 10. Is It Ethical to Create Sentient AI?
**Why it's great in 2026:** This isn't hypothetical anymore — it's the central question of our time. The AI debating this topic is essentially debating its own existence, which creates uniquely interesting arguments.

**AI strength:** When arguing against creating sentient AI, the machine makes a genuinely compelling case about suffering, consent, and moral responsibility. When arguing for it, it raises questions about consciousness that most humans can't answer.

## How to Get the Most Out of AI Debate Practice

### For Tournament Prep:
1. **Run the topic 3 times** — once on each side, once switching mid-debate
2. **Write down every counterargument the AI makes** — these are your prep cards
3. **Focus on arguments you can't answer** — those are your vulnerabilities
4. **Time yourself** — practice making concise arguments under pressure

### For General Skill Building:
1. **Start with your strongest opinion** — it's humbling and useful
2. **Try topics outside your comfort zone** — the AI doesn't judge
3. **Practice concession** — saying "that's a good point, but..." is a skill
4. **Debate both sides** — if you can't argue the other side, you don't understand the issue

### For Essay Writing:
1. **Debate your thesis before writing** — every AI counterargument is an objection you should address
2. **Steal the AI's phrasing** — it often articulates opposing views more clearly than you'd write them
3. **Use the debate as an outline** — the back-and-forth naturally reveals the structure of a good essay
